# $moral-parallax

Speculative story design for authors who want to expose how comfort, privilege, and harm stay connected even when characters try not to see it.

## Use When

- A story is about systemic harm, exported consequences, or complicity.
- The author wants a cleaner way to design moral distance and then collapse it.
- The setting needs a mechanism that makes invisible costs personal.

## Do Not Use When

- The story depends on clear moral innocence or simple villainy.
- The request is mainly about ordinary character denial without systemic distance.
- The author wants a neat, consequence-free resolution.

## Edda Workflow

1. Read the relevant Story Text, Chapter notes, and any world rules involved.
2. Identify which moral distance the story is maintaining and who benefits from it.
3. Design the collapse event, the complicity map, and the no-clean-exit problem.
4. Check that the mechanism fits the world and the emotional promise.
5. Draft or revise only when the author explicitly asks for applied story work.

## Edda Output Handling

- Return the distance-collapse design in chat by default.
- Create an Attached Note when the work belongs to one Chapter or one focused concept problem.
- Create or update a Project Note when the author wants a larger thematic plan.
- Propose Story Bible updates only if the world mechanism becomes durable canon the author wants recorded.
- Use Structured Writes only when the author explicitly asks to draft or rewrite selected Story Text.

## Script Compatibility

This source skill has no required helper scripts. The Milestone 3.5 rewrite works through Edda-native thematic analysis, concept design, and optional drafting support.
