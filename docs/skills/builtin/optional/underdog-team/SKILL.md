# $underdog-team

Team-story planning for authors who want misfits, institutional rejects, or lightly resourced crews forced into high-pressure cooperation.

## Use When

- The story depends on an outcast team inside or adjacent to a larger institution.
- Resource pressure, mandate pressure, and team friction should all matter.
- The author wants stronger team roles, constraints, and escalation.

## Do Not Use When

- The story is built around one protagonist rather than a unit.
- The team already has too many advantages or too little friction.
- The request is mainly about national politics or world systems instead of the team.

## Writer Workflow

1. Read the relevant Story Text, team notes, and any institutional context.
2. Define the mandate, the constraints, and why this group ended up together.
3. Build team roles with real friction, uneven trust, and complementary pressure points.
4. Map how success creates new problems instead of easy validation.
5. Draft or revise only when the author explicitly asks for applied story work.

## Writer Output Handling

- Return the team design in chat by default.
- Create an Attached Note when the work belongs to one Chapter cluster or one team problem.
- Create or update a Project Note when the author wants a reusable unit plan.
- Propose Story Bible updates only if the team, institution, or mandate becomes durable canon the author wants tracked.
- Use Structured Writes only when the author explicitly asks to draft or rewrite selected Story Text.

## Script Compatibility

This source skill has no required helper scripts. The Milestone 3.5 rewrite works through Writer-native team design and revision guidance.
